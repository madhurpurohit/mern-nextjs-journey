Biography of Nikola Tesla

Early life

Nikola Tesla was born on July 10, 1856, in Smiljan, which was part of the Australian Empire (modern-day Croatia). His father was a priest, and his mother was an inventor who encouraged his curiosity from a young age.

Career

Tesla moved to the United States in 1884 to work for Thomas Edison. He later invented alternating current(A.C.) electrical system, which laid the foundation for modern electricity distribution. He also conducted pioneering experiments in wireless communication and X-rays.

Achievements

Tesla held over 300 patents during his lifetime. He is best known for the contributions to the development of AC power, the Tesla coil, and wireless technologies. Despite his many contributions, Tesla faced financial difficulties later in life.

Legacy

Tesla's work has left a lasting impact on modern technologies. His inventions have proved the way for many of the electrical system we use today. He is remembered as a visionary who was ahead of time.
